Consumer

Consumers may not have much music experience but have a general interest in music gadgets. They usually have a limited budget for a device just for playing with like a toy. They may care more about its aesthetics. The interaction needs to be fun for them in order to keep them interested.

Prosumer

Prosumers are experienced in music than consumers who buy the product just for fun. They hope the device can be a tool which helps them become professional. They might tend to buy a product which looks like a tool rather than a toy. More importantly, OrBit needs to give them useful feedback and allow them to interact in a meaningful way.

Professional

As music professionals/performing artists, precise control over their performance is very important. Therefore, their requirements on functionality are higher than any other user groups. The device must be robust and reliable, so that they can use it for their performance without any ambiguity. LOGOS

Industrial Design User Feedback

After the first batch of low-fidelity prototypes were made, feedbacks were collected to understand where instrumentalists like to put the device, so that the design of attachement can be done to meet their needs.

What we learned from this testing:

  • Almost all the interviewees are willing to attach the device to their instruments
  • They are happy with using suction cups as a method of attachment
  • Some classical musicians are not interested in digital music at all
  • Majority of the interviewees chose to put the device on the feet
  • Drummers could put the device on their heads or left foot
