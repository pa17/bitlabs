Market Overview

This first section focuses on presenting the segmentation of the music market, and the respective market sizes of its subgroups, as presented below. BitLabs has positioned its first product OrBit in the music production market, which shows a fast annual growth. Moreover, integration of the product into the rapidly expanding market of music composition software leverages much of the potential this submarket offers.

Music Composing Software

The global music composing software market was valued at $79.2 million in 2017 and is forecast to witness a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 21.4% during 2018–2023. (P&S Market Research, 2018)

Music Instrument Retail

The music instrument retail industry revenue is estimated to increase at a CAGR of 2.8% to reach £458.4 million in 2019. (IBIS World, 2018)

Music Production Software

Analysts forecast the global music production software market to grow at a CAGR of 8.65% during the period 2018-2022.


Technological advances in the music industry have led to the emergence of advanced mobile-based software, which is used to mix and compose tracks. The growing preference for live events and EDM has led to an increase in the number of DJs worldwide. (Business Wire, 2018)

Through market research, BitLabs identified a growing trend in both music composition and production market, as well as a growing preference for live events. There is a potential market need for a device which can be used across these three areas, and this is what OrBit can do.

User Analysis

User Segmentation

The music production user base is heavily segmented into subgroups including everything from begineers to professionals. Moreover, producing music does not neccessarily mean playing an instrument, but also includes sound engineers and producers. An initial user base segmentation focussed on grouping this fragmented base into nine sugroups as presented below.

User Base Segmentation

Users can be categorised in terms of level of expertise and type of musician

BitLabs chose to narrow down its target market to performer segment early on. OrBit aims at augmenting primarily the performance through digital elements, and whilst also being interesting to use in a studio setting, is designed for live music specifically. The three different types of users in the performer segment are described below.

Consumer

Consumers may not have much music experience but have a general interest in music gadgets. They usually have a limited budget for a device just for playing with like a toy. They may care more about its aesthetics. The interaction needs to be fun for them in order to keep them interested.

Prosumer

Prosumers are experienced in music than consumers who buy the product just for fun. They hope the device can be a tool which helps them become professional. They might tend to buy a product which looks like a tool rather than a toy. More importantly, OrBit needs to give them useful feedback and allow them to interact in a meaningful way.

Professional

As music professionals/performing artists, precise control over their performance is very important. Therefore, their requirements on functionality are higher than any other user groups. The device must be robust and reliable, so that they can use it for their performance without any ambiguity.

User Preference Analysis

The three user subgroups identified were then analysed individually in terms of requirements and aspirations. Whilst a consumer is looking for more of a toy and is happy with a 'gimmicky' product with a lot of input and output, a professional user needs exact control and as little distraction as possible. Finally, a prosumer is located somewhere between the two. A prosumer will aspire to own a product that can take them to the professional level and ease the barrier to entry to using professional music production and performance techniques.

User Preference Analysis

BitLabs has carefully analysed the current market and identified which product characteristics are most important to the user subgroups.

From primary (shown below) and secondary research BitLabs decided to enter the prosumer market. This is because the ratio between spending power and functionality as well as the market size are the most favourable. The spending power of consumers would not be aligned with the hardware required for OrBit. Conversely, professional musicians require a level of control which would be unrealistic to achieve with a first product. Our business model (refer to Investor section), however, still aims to excite musicians of all levels of expertise through a freely available mobile demo application. In this way, BitLabs aims to speak to all branches of the heavily segmented music market and encourage consumers to shift their buying behaviour to ones of prosumers.

The Competition

BitLabs compared existing products in the digital music market. OrBit aims at a high level of interactivity and a price at a mid range. The most direct competitors to BitLabs are MIDI ring controllers such as Wave and Enhancia rings. Both of them are early-stage startups. OrBit are unique as it has much more versatile means of attachment, which allows higher level of customisation.

Musician Survey

After the first batch of low-fidelity prototypes were made, feedbacks were collected to understand where instrumentalists like to put the device, so that the design of attachement can be done to meet their needs.

What we learned from this testing:

  • Almost all the interviewees are willing to attach the device to their instruments
  • They are happy with using suction cups as a method of attachment
  • Some classical musicians are not interested in digital music at all
  • Majority of the interviewees chose to put the device on the feet
  • Drummers could put the device on their heads or left foot
